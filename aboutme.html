Background

I grew up in metro Detroit and will always consider that area to be my home. Since then, I have spent most of my time in other Michigan cities - with the exception of a semester abroad in the United Kingdom at the University of Derby during my undergraduate career.

In 2011, I received a BS from Eastern Michigan University with majors in mathematics and general science and a minor in economics. After that, I moved to Mount Pleasant and began working on a Ph.D. in Mathematics with a concentration in the teaching of college mathematics at CMU. I successfully defended my dissertation in September 2017, and I currently work as an Accelerate mathematics instructor at The South Carolina Governor's School for Science & Mathematics.

Math-Related Activities

I make it a priority to be an active member of whatever mathematics community I am currently a part of. In particular, I have been involved with the following student organizations:

  • AMS Graduate Student Chapter: Founding Treasurer during the Spring 2015 semester, and President from Fall 2015 until Spring 2017. (If the website design seems familiar, it's because I built that one too.)
  • Math Club at EMU: Founding Vice President during my junior year, and then President until I graduated in Spring 2011.

I'm also affiliated with the following professional organizations:

  • American Mathematical Society (AMS): member since 2010.
  • Mathematical Association of America (MAA): member since 2015.
  • Society of Industrial and Applied Mathematics (SIAM): member since 2013.
  • The National Consortium of Secondary STEM Schools (NCSSS): member since 2017.

Other Activities

Frankly, I think I might go crazy if all I did was teach and reseach mathematics. In an effort to avoid a psychiatric institution, I make time for other activites.

Here are some of the highlights:

  • Common Ground: Common Ground is SCGSSM's gender and sexual minorities organization. I don't serve as their official faculty advisor, but I do attend their meetings and contribute whenever I am able to do so.
  • Central Harmony A Cappella: I sang in CMU's co-ed competitive a cappella group Central Harmony for three years, serving as its Business Manager for two of those years.
  • Community Theater: I've performed in shows at the Broadway Theatre in Mount Pleasant, the Chelsea Area Players in Chelsea, and as part of the (now defunct) Beneath the Trap Door Production company in metro Detroit.
  • Choral Singing: While at EMU, I spent time singing in the University Choir, the Chamber Choir, and the Vocal Jazz Group.
  • Boy Scouts of America: Although I'm no longer active, I am a proud Eagle Scout.
